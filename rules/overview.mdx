## What is Yokai Septet Online?

Yokai Septet Online is a team-based trick-taking card game for 4 players. Two teams of 2 players compete to collect sevens or win tricks, with strategic card swapping and trump mechanics adding depth to every round.

## Game Structure

<Steps>
  <Step title="Form Teams">
    Four players split into two teams of 2 players each. Teammates sit opposite each other at the table.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Deal Cards">
    Each player receives 12 cards from a 49-card deck. One card is set aside as the trump card.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Card Swap Phase">
    Players select 3 cards to swap with their partner across the table.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Play Tricks">
    Players take turns playing cards in tricks. The winner of each trick leads the next one.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Score the Round">
    The round ends when a team wins by collecting sevens or tricks. Points are awarded based on the sevens collected.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Win Conditions

A round can end in several ways:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Collect 4+ Sevens">
    If your team collects 4 or more sevens (cards with rank 7), the round ends immediately. Your team scores points based on the sevens you collected.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Win 7+ Tricks">
    If your team wins 7 or more tricks, the round ends. The **opposing team** scores points for their sevens, plus any sevens remaining in your team's hands.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="All 12 Tricks Played">
    If all 12 tricks are played without either team reaching 4 sevens or 7 tricks, the team of the final trick winner scores points for their sevens.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Info>
  The game continues across multiple rounds. The first team to reach the target score wins the game.
</Info>

## Key Mechanics

### Trump Suit

The trump card determines the trump suit for the entire round. Trump cards beat all non-trump cards in a trick, except for the special Ace.

### The Ace Rule

The Green Ace (card 1) is the most powerful card in the game. It automatically wins any trick it's played in, regardless of trump or lead suit.

### Following Suit

Players must follow the lead suit if they have a card of that suit in their hand. If they don't have the lead suit, they can play any card, including trump.

### Team Seating

Teammates always sit opposite each other. The seating arrangement is: Team 1, Team 2, Team 1, Team 2.

<Note>
  Understanding the scoring system is crucial. A team that collects sevens scores points, but winning too many tricks gives points to the opponent!
</Note>
